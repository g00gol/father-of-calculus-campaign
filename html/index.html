Introduction to the Father of Calculus Campaign

The calculus controversy is an argument of the initial discovery of calculus. While calculus was inherently formulated by Archimedes, a Greek mathematician, Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz and Sir Isaac Newton are argued to have founded modern calculus. This spurs much of the controversy as the public insisted to select only one candidate as its true founder.
